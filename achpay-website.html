Frequently Asked Questions

Questions from finance teams

Is ACH safe for large transactions? +
Yes. ACH is the payment rail used by the Federal Reserve, the US Treasury, and virtually every bank in the United States. In 2023, the ACH Network processed over $80 trillion in payments. ACH is appropriate for any transaction size, and ACHPay adds additional layers of verification, fraud screening, and audit controls on top of the base network.
How does the $5 maximum fee work? +
ACHPay charges a percentage-based fee with a hard cap of $5.00 per transaction. On smaller transactions, you may pay less than $5 — but you will never pay more, regardless of transaction size. A $10,000 invoice and a $10,000,000 invoice both incur a maximum fee of $5.00. This pricing is contractual and appears explicitly in your service agreement.
How long does ACH settlement take? +
Standard ACH settles in 1–2 business days. Same-Day ACH is available on our Growth and Enterprise plans, settling within hours on the same business day for transactions submitted before the NACHA cutoff. ACHPay also provides provisional credit visibility so your treasury team can plan cash positions before funds are fully settled.
Can my customers be required to pay by ACH? +
Yes, particularly in B2B contexts. Many enterprise companies require ACH or wire for invoices over a defined threshold. ACHPay supports mandate collection, ACH authorization workflows, and both pull (debit) and push (credit) payment flows depending on your business model and customer agreement structure.
What happens when a payment fails or is returned? +
ACHPay provides real-time webhook notifications for all payment events including failures, NSF returns, and unauthorized returns. Our dashboard surfaces return codes with plain-language explanations, and our retry engine can automatically resubmit eligible transactions under configurable rules. Return handling is a first-class feature, not an afterthought.
How quickly can we go live? +
Most teams are processing test transactions within 48 hours of onboarding. Production go-live typically takes 5–10 business days, including bank account verification, underwriting, and compliance review. Enterprise customers with complex integration requirements can work with our Solutions Engineering team for a guided implementation.

How It Works

Up and running in days, not months

ACHPay is designed to fit into your existing financial stack — not replace it. Implementation is fast, secure, and guided by our team.

01
Onboarding & underwriting
Submit your business and banking information. Our compliance team reviews your application — typically within 24–48 hours. We check entity verification, business purpose, and transaction profile to ensure smooth ODFI sponsorship. For enterprises, a dedicated onboarding manager guides the entire process.
02
API integration or no-code setup
Most teams integrate via our REST API using official SDKs for Node.js, Python, Ruby, Go, and Java. If you prefer a no-code setup, the ACHPay dashboard supports manual payment initiation, bulk CSV uploads, and workflow automation without writing a line of code. Our sandbox environment mirrors production exactly, so testing is deterministic.
03
Connect your bank accounts
Link your bank accounts via Instant Account Verification (IAV) — a real-time connection that confirms ownership and routing details without test deposits. For enterprise configurations, micro-deposit verification is also available. Multiple accounts and entities are supported under a single ACHPay organization.
04
Initiate your first payment
With a single API call or dashboard action, initiate an ACH debit or credit. Specify the amount, entry class (CCD, PPD, etc.), settlement speed, and any reference data for reconciliation. ACHPay queues and transmits your file to the ODFI at the next available NACHA window.
05
Settlement and reconciliation
As payments settle, ACHPay updates status in real time via webhooks and the dashboard. Our reconciliation engine matches settled amounts to your reference data automatically. Any returns are surfaced immediately with the NACHA return code, plain-language explanation, and retry options.
06
Reporting & optimization
Access daily settlement reports, transaction-level audit logs, and monthly fee summaries — all exportable for your ERP or accounting software. Our analytics surface return rates, settlement timing patterns, and payment success metrics so your team can continually optimize payment operations.
